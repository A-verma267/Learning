The Earth is our home planet, and it's a unique and fascinating place. The consequences of a warming climate are far-reaching and are already threatening some people's ways of life and damaging wider biodiversity. If Earth becomes uninhabitable, we have nowhere else to go. Colonizing the Moon and Mars is no substitute for preserving Earth. The Moon and Mars cannot sustain Earth's population of humans and other organisms. The Earth's internal structure consists of a core, mantle, and crust. The core is about 4,400 miles (7,100 km) wide, slightly larger than half the Earth's diameter, and about the same size as Mars. The outermost 1,400 miles (2,250 km) of the core are liquid, while the inner core is solid. The core is responsible for the planet's magnetic field, which helps to deflect harmful charged particles shot from the sun. Above the core is the mantle, which is about 1,800 miles (2,900 km) thick. The mantle is not completely stiff but can flow slowly. The Earth's crust floats on the mantle much as a piece of wood floats on water. The slow motion of rock in the mantle shuffles continents around and causes earthquakes, volcanoes, and the formation of mountain ranges. The Earth's crust is divided into two kinds: the dry land of the continents, which consists mostly of granite and other light silicate minerals, and the ocean floors, which are made up mostly of a dark, dense volcanic rock called basalt. The atmosphere surrounds the Earth, with the lowest layer being the troposphere, which is constantly in motion and responsible for our weather. Above the troposphere is the stratosphere, which contains the ozone layer that protects us from harmful ultraviolet radiation. The atmosphere also traps heat from the sun, warming the Earth through the greenhouse effect. The Earth's chemical composition is unique, with the right distance from the sun and the right chemistry to support liquid water and life. The planet's surface is dynamic, with processes like plate tectonics shaping the Earth's surface over time. Overall, the Earth is a remarkable planet that supports a vast array of life forms, and it's essential that we work to preserve it for future generations
